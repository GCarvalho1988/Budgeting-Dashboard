This is a departure from my usual posts, so bear with me.

I've signed myself up for something. Manchester to London. 220 miles. 28 June. For Ambitious about Autism.

I've never done anything like this before. Not the distance, and not the asking. 220 miles is a long way at the best of times. Seven weeks after Fred Whitton it's something else. Fred Whitton is 112 miles through the Lake District, the hardest sportive in the country. I signed up for that one too, because apparently I don't learn.

I have a full-time job, a family, and a calendar that doesn't obviously have space for the training this requires. The next 13 weeks are going to be interesting.

Why this cause? Simon Mottram, Rapha's founder, backs this ride every year. Quietly, consistently. That kind of commitment from someone you respect tells you something.

Target is £1,000. I'll post updates as it unfolds. The training, the juggling act, the honest bits. Link in the first comment 🚴
